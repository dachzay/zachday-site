Martin

Martin is a personal AI system I built to turn messy inputs—notes, ideas, signals—into clear next actions.

The problem

Most work breaks down not because of a lack of information, but because context is scattered. Notes live in one place, signals in another, decisions in your head.

Martin exists to reduce that friction—to help me think clearly, prioritize correctly, and move from ambiguity to action faster.

What Martin produces

Martin doesn’t generate ideas for me. It helps me decide what to do next.

  • Clear action plans
  • Prioritized next steps
  • Drafts I can actually send
  • Summaries that reduce cognitive load

How I use it

Martin is part of my daily workflow. I feed it raw context—notes, tasks, signals— and use its outputs as a thinking partner, not an authority.

It’s designed to support judgment, not replace it.

What’s next

This page documents Martin at a high level. Over time, I’ll add deeper write-ups on how it works, what it’s made of, and what it’s taught me about building usable AI systems.
