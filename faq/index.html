FAQ for owner-led SMB AI implementation.

These are the most common questions before submitting a scope request.

How is pricing structured?

Pricing is scoped to implementation complexity and pilot boundaries, not generic strategy retainers. Intro calls are used to define workflow scope, timeline, and delivery requirements before final quoting.

How long does a first pilot usually take?

Most owner-led SMB pilots run 3 to 4 weeks from diagnosis to handoff. The exact timeline depends on workflow complexity, system access, and review availability from your team.

What bandwidth does my team need to provide?

At minimum: one owner for decisions, one operator for workflow validation, and consistent access to the systems involved in the pilot. Without named ownership, rollout quality declines.

How do you handle data and privacy?

Only the data required for scoped workflows is used. Validation checkpoints and explicit guardrails are applied to reduce unreliable outputs. Sensitive customer and internal details are not published in proof materials.

Will this work for my business size?

Best fit is owner-led SMB teams with recurring manual workflows and willingness to implement operating changes. If your team is still deciding whether to execute at all, start with constraints review on the Process page first.
